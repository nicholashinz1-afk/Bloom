Best Posting Times

Suggested times are in your audience's local time zone (default to EST if unsure). Mental health content performs best during quiet, reflective moments, not peak scroll hours.

Weekdays (best)
9:00 – 11:00 AM
Morning scroll. Highest engagement window.
Weekdays (alt)
7:00 – 8:00 PM
Evening wind-down. Good for emotional content.
Weekends
10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Slower scroll, but higher saves and shares.
Crisis/resource posts
6:00 – 8:00 PM
Late-day posts get saved more. Reach people when they need it.

Once you have 100+ followers, check Instagram Insights > Audience > Most Active Times. Your actual data beats any generic advice.

Engagement Types

Each post is tagged with the kind of engagement it's designed to drive. This isn't about gaming the algorithm. It's about understanding what each post does so you can lean into it.

Save-driven
Educational or resource content people bookmark for later. Saves are the strongest algorithm signal on Instagram. These posts build long-term reach.
Share-driven
Values-based content people send to friends or repost in Stories. Shares expand your audience beyond your followers. This is how you grow organically.
Comment-driven
Relatable or emotional content that makes people want to respond. Comments boost visibility and build community. Reply to every comment in the first hour.
Swipe-driven
Carousel posts designed to make people swipe. Swipe-through rate is a strong signal. Bait-and-switch openers and curiosity gaps drive this.

General Playbook

First hour matters most. Reply to every comment and DM within 60 minutes of posting. Instagram's algorithm decides reach in the first hour based on early engagement velocity.

Stories come second, not simultaneously. Post the feed post first. Wait 15-60 minutes, then post the Story companion. The feed post needs its own engagement window (comments, saves) before you drive attention to Stories. If you post both at once, people see the Story first and skip the feed post entirely. Each post below has a specific Story timing suggestion based on content type.

Don't sell. Show up. Bloom's content philosophy is the same as its design philosophy: no pressure, no shame. Don't beg for follows, don't use "link in bio" in every caption (only where it's natural), and don't post engagement bait that feels hollow. People can tell when something is real. Be real.

Consistency over virality. Posting every 2 days matters more than any single post going viral. The algorithm rewards accounts that show up reliably. This calendar is designed for that rhythm.

Let people feel seen. The mental health niche is full of accounts that talk at people. Bloom talks with them. When someone comments something vulnerable, honor that. A genuine two-word reply beats a paragraph.
