# Why Bloom Exists

Bloom exists because mental health tools should be free, private, and gentle. Built by someone who's been through it, for people going through it. No company, no investors, no monetization. Just a quiet place to show up for yourself.

## The North Star

A real user once shared that Bloom's weekly insight made them emotional. Not because it was clever or clinical, but because it saw them. It named their struggles honestly and reflected back the quiet strength in the small things they were already doing. Journaling once. Two breathing exercises. A shower on a hard week. It told them those things mattered.

That is the feeling we are protecting. Every feature, every line of copy, every design decision should be measured against that moment. Did we make someone feel less alone? Did we help them see what they're already doing right?

## What We're Building For

- People who are going through it right now
- People who can't afford therapy or don't have access
- People who need a gentle nudge, not a lecture
- People who just need one thing to tell them: what you did today was enough

## What We Will Never Do

- Sell, store, or exploit user data
- Monetize access to core features
- Use clinical or corporate tone
- Pressure users with streaks, guilt, or shame
- Over-filter emotional language (venting is healthy)
- Pretend this replaces professional care

## The Standard

If a feature doesn't serve someone on their hardest day, it doesn't ship. If the tone wouldn't feel right coming from a friend who's been there, rewrite it. If it helps even one person feel a little less alone, it was worth building.

Bloom will always be free.
