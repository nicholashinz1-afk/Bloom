## The full story

I'm Nick, a Detroit native still living in the metro area. I'm an Interlochen Arts Academy alum ('09) and DePauw University grad ('14). I work in tech for a Michigan distributor. I'm a husband and a dad of three. I also live with MS.

In 2025, I went through a partial hospitalization program in Troy, MI. If you've never been through one, it's basically structured outpatient therapy five days a week. You sit in a room with other people who are also having the hardest stretch of their lives, and you work on staying alive and functional. It's humbling and it's honest and it saved my life.

Towards the end of the program, we worked on co-regulation cards. These are cards you can hand to someone you trust when you're escalated or in crisis, so they know how to help you when you can't articulate it yourself. We also talked a lot about habits. How hard it is to shower when you're depressed. How hard it is to drink water, go outside, move your body. Not because you don't know you should, but because the weight of everything makes the simplest things feel impossible.

That's when I decided to build Bloom. Not a company. Not a product. Not something to monetize or scale or pitch to investors. Just a quiet, free place where someone going through it could track their mood, write down what they're feeling, build small habits, and breathe. A place that doesn't punish you for missing a day. A place that doesn't push motivational garbage at you when you're barely holding on. A place that meets you where you are.

I taught myself to build it. Every feature in Bloom comes from something I needed or something someone in that room needed. Mood tracking exists because naming how you feel is one of the most effective things you can do for emotional regulation, and we practiced it every single day in PHP. Journaling exists because expressive writing helped me process things I couldn't say out loud. Hard day mode exists because some days all you can do is two things, and that should be enough. Crisis resources are always one tap away because I know what it's like to need them.

Bloom is open source, free, and always will be. No accounts, no data collection, no ads. Everything stays on your device. I built it for people like the people I sat next to in that room. People who deserve tools that don't exploit them, don't shame them, and don't cost money they don't have.

I'm not trying to start a movement or build a brand. I'm a husband and a dad of three who just wants to do something that helps people who are going through what I've gone through. If Bloom makes one person's hard day a little easier, it was worth building.
