How Bloom Keeps People Safe

People of all ages use Bloom. The safety infrastructure reflects that responsibility.

Our Approach

Bloom is a mental health self-care app. People use it on hard days, when they're anxious, when they need somewhere to be honest about how they're feeling. That means safety can't be an afterthought.

Every message sent through Bloom's community features is automatically screened before it reaches anyone else. Venting is always allowed. Difficult emotions are always allowed. Language that targets or harms other people is not.

All of this is open source. You can read every moderation rule, every pattern, every line of code. Nothing is hidden behind a policy document you can't verify.

Age Protection

Bloom uses a three-tier age gate during onboarding. No account is created, but community access depends on the age group selected.

Under 13
Cannot use Bloom. Shown crisis resources and directed to a trusted adult.
13 to 15
Solo mode only. Journaling, habits, breathing exercises, and crisis resources. No community features.
16+
Full access including anonymous buddy system and community encouragement wall.

Content Moderation

Every message in the buddy system and encouragement wall is checked against a shared moderation module before it's delivered. Here's what happens at each tier.

Blocked

Credible Threats of Violence

Messages describing specific, actionable threats (mass violence, named targets, weapons combined with intent) are blocked immediately. These are logged with full metadata for compliance and trigger real-time admin alerts via email and push notification.

Blocked

Directed Harm

Messages intended to hurt another person, including telling someone to harm themselves, death threats, or targeted cruelty. Blocked in both buddy and wall.

Blocked

Grooming and Predatory Language

Questions about age, grade, location, or school. Requests for photos or selfies. Secrecy language ("don't tell anyone," "keep this between us"). Requests to meet in person. All blocked unconditionally.

Blocked

Contact Exchange

Phone numbers, social media handles, and any attempt to move communication off-platform. Blocked on both buddy and wall. The client also warns users before they send personal information, with a two-step confirmation.

Blocked

Targeted Abuse

Slurs and language used to dehumanize others. Blocked everywhere.

Blocked in buddy / Flagged on wall

Crude and Sexual Content

Hard-blocked in the buddy system (1-on-1 messages between users). On the public encouragement wall, this content is allowed but shown with a content warning so others can choose whether to view it.

Blocked

Spam and Links

URLs, special characters used for injection, and spam patterns are blocked to prevent abuse.

Allowed, with resources

Self-Harm Language

This is never blocked. If someone is expressing suicidal thoughts or self-harm, their message goes through. Bloom surfaces crisis resources (988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, Crisis Text Line, international helplines) alongside the message. Better to show help to someone who doesn't need it than to miss someone who does.

Automatic Enforcement

Auto-Ban

Three or more blocked messages within 24 hours triggers an automatic ban from all community features. No admin intervention needed. Credible violent threats result in an immediate ban on the first offense.

Threat Response

Compliance Logging and Admin Alerts

When a credible threat of violence is detected, Bloom logs the full incident (timestamp, message content, IP address, user agent, and user identifier) and immediately notifies administrators through three channels: email alert via Resend, push notification, and the admin console.

Bloom maintains a documented incident response plan with severity tiers. For credible, imminent threats, the plan includes cooperation with law enforcement. This is disclosed in Bloom's Terms of Use.

Privacy by Design

Your Data Stays on Your Device

Journals, mood logs, habit data, and settings are stored in your browser's local storage and IndexedDB. Nothing is sent to a server. There are no user accounts and no sign-up process.

AI Reflections

When you use AI-powered journal reflections, your entry is sent to the Anthropic Claude API for processing. Bloom does not store your journal entries on any server. Anthropic's data retention policies apply to API calls.

Community Data

If you opt into community features (buddy system, encouragement wall), messages are stored on the server with no identifying information beyond the display name you choose. Community data can be deleted at any time from Settings.

No Tracking

No analytics platforms. No tracking pixels. No advertising. No third-party data sharing. Bloom uses batched, anonymous telemetry for basic operational metrics (page loads, feature usage counts) with no personally identifiable information.

Client-Side Personal Info Warnings

Before sending any community message, Bloom scans for phone numbers, email addresses, social security numbers, physical addresses, and social media handles. If detected, you see a warning and must confirm before the message is sent. The server blocks personal information regardless of client-side confirmation.

Personal Safety Features

Not everyone using Bloom is in a safe environment. These features are designed for people who need to protect their privacy from someone in their life, or who need help but can't openly look for it.

Privacy Lock

Users can lock the app behind a PIN or their device's biometrics (fingerprint, Face ID, device PIN). The lock activates when the app is opened and when returning from the background, with a configurable timeout.

The lock screen is minimal and doesn't display any personal data. Crisis resources (the white heart button) remain accessible on the lock screen, including the discreet safety resources via long-press.

PIN is hashed with SHA-256 and stored locally. The threat model is casual snooping, not forensic analysis. If a user chooses biometrics, a backup PIN is also required in case biometrics aren't available.

Discreet Safety Resources

Long-pressing the white heart button in the header for three seconds opens a separate set of resources for people in unsafe situations. These are distinct from the standard crisis resources (which focus on self-harm and suicide).

The safety resources include:

  • National Domestic Violence Hotline (thehotline.org, live chat 24/7)
  • RAINN (online.rainn.org, sexual assault support chat 24/7)
  • Love Is Respect (loveisrespect.org, teen and young adult dating abuse chat)
  • Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline (1-800-422-4453)
  • Crisis Text Line (web chat option)
  • International resources via findahelpline.com

Web chat links are listed first because they don't appear in call or text history. Phone numbers are available but clearly labeled "if you're in a safe place to call."

Quick Exit (Decoy Screen)

The safety resources screen includes a Quick Exit button that instantly covers the entire app with a neutral "loading" overlay. It looks like the app froze. No one looking at the screen would suspect anything.

To return, the user long-presses the loading spinner for three seconds. The session stays alive underneath. If the user was in the middle of a chat with a hotline, that conversation is preserved.

This pattern is adapted from domestic violence resource websites, which commonly include a "quick escape" button. The decoy approach is better for a PWA because navigating away would kill any active chat session.

Discoverability

The long-press trigger is intentionally hidden so that someone going through a user's phone won't find these resources. Users learn about it through the What's New tour on version update, or through a subtle hint in the FAQ section of Settings: "The white heart also has more resources if you hold it."

Technical Safeguards

  • Content Security Policy (CSP) restricting script and resource origins
  • X-Frame-Options: DENY (prevents embedding in iframes)
  • Strict-Transport-Security with preload (HTTPS only)
  • X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff
  • Referrer-Policy: strict-origin-when-cross-origin
  • CORS locked to bloomselfcare.app, the Vercel preview domain, and localhost for development

Data Deletion

Local data (journals, moods, habits): Clear your browser storage at any time. Bloom provides a one-tap reset option in Settings.

Community data (buddy messages, wall posts): Deletable from Settings at any time.

International users: Contact bloomhabits@proton.me for any data removal requests.

Crisis Resources

Always One Tap Away

The white heart button in Bloom's header opens crisis resources at any time, from any screen. No navigation, no login, no barriers. These resources are accessible even when the app is locked.

988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988, US)

Crisis Text Line (text 741741, US/Canada/UK/Ireland)

findahelpline.com (international, 40+ languages)

Crisis resources are also automatically surfaced when self-harm language is detected in community messages, and when a user logs a low mood.

Long-pressing the heart for three seconds opens a separate set of discreet safety resources for people in unsafe environments (see Personal Safety Features above).
