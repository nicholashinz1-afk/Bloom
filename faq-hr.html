Frequently Asked Questions for HR & Compliance

These questions come up when organizations evaluate Bloom as a wellness resource. The short version: Bloom collects nothing, costs nothing, and requires nothing from IT.

Privacy & Compliance

Is Bloom HIPAA-compliant?
HIPAA does not apply. Bloom is not a covered entity and collects no protected health information (PHI). All data stays on the employee's device. Nothing is transmitted to or stored on Bloom's servers.
Do we need a Business Associate Agreement (BAA)?
No. A BAA is only required when a vendor handles PHI on your behalf. Bloom never receives, processes, or stores any employee health data.
Can we see what data Bloom collects?
Zero data is collected. No accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no cookies for identification. The entire source code is public and auditable at github.com/nicholashinz1-afk/bloom.
Can managers or HR see employee usage?
No. There is no admin dashboard, no usage reporting, and no way to identify who uses Bloom. It is completely anonymous by design.
What about the AI journal reflections?
When an employee chooses to use AI reflections, only the journal text is sent to generate a response. Nothing is stored, logged, or linked to any identity. This feature is optional and off by default.

Cost & Logistics

What does Bloom cost?
Nothing. Free forever. No licensing fees, no per-seat pricing, no contracts, no premium tier. Bloom is open source (AGPL-3.0) and will never be monetized.
What does IT need to do?
Nothing. Bloom runs in any web browser, no download required. No SSO integration, no provisioning. Employees visit bloomselfcare.app and start using it. There is nothing to maintain or update.
Is this a replacement for our EAP?
No. Bloom is a daily self-care companion, not therapy. It works alongside your EAP as a low-barrier tool employees can use on their own. The app includes a permanent disclaimer and always-accessible crisis resources (988, Crisis Text Line).
How do we share it with employees?
Share the link (bloomselfcare.app) or QR code in a wellness newsletter, benefits page, intranet, or awareness event. That's all it takes. No onboarding, no enrollment.
What if Bloom goes away?
Bloom is open source. Even if the hosted version disappeared, the code is permanently public and anyone can run it. Employee data is unaffected regardless, since it lives only on their own device.
