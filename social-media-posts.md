## LinkedIn Post

Last year, I walked out of a partial hospitalization program and started building Bloom.

Not a startup. Not a pitch deck. Just a thing I needed that didn't exist.

Bloom is a mental health self-care app. It tracks mood, habits, journaling, and sleep. It offers AI-powered reflections that are warm and grounded, never clinical. It has crisis resources one tap away. And it has a hard day mode that makes the entire app gentler when you're struggling.

Here's what makes it different:

- Free forever. No premium tier, no trial period, no "upgrade to unlock."
- Completely private. No accounts. No tracking. All data stays on the device.
- Open source (AGPL-3.0). Anyone can read the code, contribute, or fork it.
- No investors, no company. Just one person who cares about this problem.

The streaks never punish you. They count total days you showed up, and they never go down. Because progress isn't a straight line, and an app shouldn't make you feel bad for being human.

Today, Bloom is available on Google Play.

Same app that's been live at bloomselfcare.app as a web app. Now it's easier to find and install on Android. If you know someone who could use a quiet, pressure-free space to take care of themselves, I'd be grateful if you shared it.

Mental health tools should be free, private, and gentle. That's the whole philosophy.
